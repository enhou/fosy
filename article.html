Journey to the West

Print   |   Email to a friend
With its loveable Monkey King, Journey to the West is possibly China's most popular novel of all time.

It endures over the centuries not just because of the crazy escapades of its fun characters, but also because it has a deeper spiritual motif of a personal journey.

The novel is one of the Four Classics of Chinese folklore, all written between the 14th and 18th centuries. Each of these books is a key to understanding different aspects of traditional Chinese culture.

Journey to the West provides insight into Buddhism and traditional Chinese beliefs in general—practically the entire pantheon of Chinese deities appears somewhere in the plot. And these are precisely the traditions Mao and the Communist Party set out to destroy, which is why, during the Cultural Revolution, this classic was banned.

Set back in the 7th century, during the great Tang Dynasty, the journey begins with the emperor handpicking a young Buddhist monk to go on a dangerous pilgrimage in search of true scriptures.
